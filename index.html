The Compliance Costs of Non-Tariff Measures: Measurement, Theory, and Policy

We use textual analysis algorithm to uncover that when imports are affected by per-shipment compliance costs created by non-tariff trade barriers, it will have a sizeable direct welfare effect on the economy.

Ritabrata Bose, Ashima Goyal
November 2020 IGIDR Working Paper 2020-033
Disaggregated Indian Industrial Cycles: A spectral analysis

First, we study the characteristics of asymmetric phase shifts and co-movement of industrial production cycles. Then we check the spectral causality of these cycles with various policy variables, which enabled us to explore the non-linear structure of the economy by computing Granger Causality for each frequency separately.
PDF Cite
Ritabrata Bose, K.V. Ramaswamy
November 2020 IGIDR Working Paper 2020-036 (Revise & Resubmit)
Globalization and Worker Composition in Indian Formal manufacturing: New Evidence on Product Market Competition Channel

We investigate the indirect impact of globalization on job quality (workforce composition) through changes in product market structure for the Indian formal manufacturing industries. We found that the firms responded to greater product market competition due to globalization by hiring relatively more contract workers. It enabled them to achieve labor market flexibility.
PDF Cite
Ritabrata Bose, Santanu Ghosh
November 2020 Empirical Econometrics and Quantitative Economics Letters (EEQEL)
Slums in Kolkata: A Socio-economic analysis

We conducted a primary survey of five urban slums in Kolkata to document their socio-economic pattern and living standards. While the slums mostly suffer from a lack of the necessary infrastructure and facilities (like the provision of hygienic toilet facilities), there are some development and modernization signs.
