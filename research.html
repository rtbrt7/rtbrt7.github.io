Research Summary

I am a prospective PhD candidate in Economics, with wide-ranging interests in Trade and Labour Economics, with special interest in the spatial analysis of these topics.

In my MPhil thesis, I have examined how globalisation could affect workforce composition (regular workers and contract workers) of industries and thereby quality of jobs, a key labour market indicator. I have studied this question by taking into account how globalisation has altered the market structure of the formal Indian manufacturing industries and how it has affected the workforce composition. The study required the combination of firm level panel data and factory level panel data, collected from two different sources.

In an inflexible labour market like India, my findings provide significant evidence of Indian firms responding to globalisation by hiring relatively more of contract workers. Our finding identifies an important indirect way in which globalisation could affect labour market conditions and worker welfare.

Abstract

Industrial cycles of India: Frequency analysis

with Ashima Goyal
Under review

Abstract

Analysis of disaggregated industrial cycles in India gives specific results on the dating and duration of industry cycles, their cyclicality, phase shifts, amplitude, duration symmetry, co-movement and coherence. Since co-movement is greater during troughs, a broad-based upturn is possible in suitable conditions. The exchange rate, currency, credit, nominal and real interest rates all affect industries, but differences in impact by industry type may be due to the structure of the economy. Causality in the frequency domain has been applied to find novel relationships for the Indian economy.

with Jeeten Giri
Working paper

Abstract

We hypothesise that this pro-female institutional change helped to increase the bargaining power of the mothers due to the psychological feeling that they now have a collateral to fall back, which leads to increase care and thus better health outcomes. Exchange Rate Elasticity of Migration: Case Study of India

Work in progress

Abstract

In this study, we have analyzed how exchange rate affects migration decision, by estimating the exchange rate elasticity of migration. We have utilised micro-founded RUM model which under special condition allows for unobserved heterogeneity between the migrants and non-migrants. Using Indian migration data from 24 OECD countries spanning from 1995 - 2014, we have found out that with a 10% real depreciation of origin country currency, there is a 3.1% to7% increase in migration flow.
